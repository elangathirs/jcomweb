Membership



Members are individuals who are either active or associate or Alumni members of JCI, admitted in JCOM and have paid the current year subscription of JCOM

One table can admit only one person from the same profession or classification.

JCI India will have the final authority relating to classifications .

One individual member can join only one live table, only one virtual table and unlimited number of association tables on payment of corresponding subscriptions.

Admission
The admission of members is a process which includes submission of online application, payment of respective subscription followed by an approval by the National Head Quarters of J COM
Fees Structure
  • For enrolling the new member, registration fees will be Rs.2000+18% GST (non- refundable) per year
  • The renewal fee of Rs 2000 + 18% GST should be paid before January 1st of every year
  • The sitting fees will be fixed by the Executive Board and may vary from table to table
  • Every member should pay the registration fees before the enrollment
  • The member has to pay the sitting fees on or before the meeting as decided by their table executive board
  • Any further expenses for the table will be collected as per the decision of the Executive Board
  • The terminated member based on the attendance can rejoin only by paying full registration fees if the executive committee of the table agrees
Attendance
  • Attendance is most essential to continue the membership
  • Maximum 6 absence are allowed in 6 months for the rolling period
  • The person failing in the above criteria to be deemed to lose their membership of JCOM
  • Once the JCOM membership is lost, the website username and password will be barred the table
  • For the virtual meetings the attendance will be counted only if the members camera is switched on during the entire meeting.
  • Once the membership is terminated he/ she cannot attend any JCOM meeting of any table.
Dress Code
The dress code for the JCOM is full formal as recommended by JCOM .
Category
Members who wish to change their category must submit a new membership application and obtain approval from the JCOM for that category change
Chairman - Roles and Responsibilities
  • Script of the Meeting Agenda with Current Status
  • Attendance List
  • Rooster Sheets, Connects and Credential Scores
  • 5 minutes Presenter Biography
  • Educational Slot Presenter
  • Mobile App Weekly Report
  • JCOM New Member Application form and their biography
  • Keep the meeting enthusiastic and Motivate by recognizing
  • To update the members about the current updates in JCOM
  • Any Current Local Organisation President/ ZGB member cannot take the post of a Chairman during his office.
Vice Chairman - Roles and Responsibilities
  • In the absence of Chairman act on the role of the chairman
  • Script of Meeting
  • making notes of the Meeting’s Proceedings
  • Identifying Achievers and Rewarding
  • Purchasing Gifts, Stationary, Printing Rooster Sheets, Agenda
Secretary Cum Treasure - Roles and Responsibilities
  • Bridge between JCOM and Table
  • Scripts of Meeting
  • Attendance Register Maintenance
  • Member’s Dues and Meeting Fee Collection
  • Payments
  • Reporting in Meeting
  • Support the President by noticing the Absence and Substitute
Directors - Roles and Responsibilities
  • In the absence of Vice Chairman or Secretary, supporting the chair
  • Introducing the 5 minute Presenter
  • Control of chapter Co-Ordinators Knowledge Education Attendance presentation.
Code of Conduct
  • The JCOM table member should maintain minimum code of conduct as expected by the Executive Board and JCOM team
  • In the absence of a Executive Board, The executive board may act as an ad- hoc Executive Board until one is established
  • JCOM Membership lists are for the purpose of 'giving' connects and not for soliciting (via e-mail, direct mail or other means) JCOM members without their prior approval.
  • All new members must attend the Member Achiever training Program in their Zone within the first 60 days of joining. Only after attending the training may the new member is added to the 'speaker rotation' for that Table. Any new members not attending the training within 60 days of induction into the Table will be subject to having their classification opened by the Executive Board
  • Other than normal JCOM printed materials, members may not use the JCOM Intellectual Property (eg. logos, trademarks, names, slogans, copyrighted materials, etc.) to manufacture, distribute, sell, market, or promote any product or service, or otherwise use the JCOM Intellectual Property without obtaining the prior written consent of JCOM HQ or the appropriate Zone Office. Members must agree to abide by the JCOM Branding Standards for any permitted use.
Termination
  • Apart from attendance criteria any member/officer of JCOM found violating or causing injuries to the JCOM can be terminated from the by issuing a Showcase notice with the 7 days time period of reply. If the reply is not found satisfactory by JCOM or by the JCOM board, the member can be terminated from JCOM. A member can be terminated directly by JCOM board
